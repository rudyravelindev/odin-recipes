Scalloped-Potatoes

Description

These Pome de terre au Gratin (scalloped potatoes) are cheesy, creamy, flavorful and oh so tender. These are different in the fact that they are smothered in the cheese sauce, and are very well coated as opposed to other recipes that layer the bare potatoes then sauce then potatoes and so on. I’ve tried it that way and I didn’t enjoy it as much as I enjoyed it this way. They’re really good and I hope you give them a try and add them to your repertoire

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs russet potatoes peeled and sliced about ½ inch thick (about 5-6 medium potatoes)
  • 4 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • ½ cup onion, diced
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons all purpose flour
  • ¾ cup chicken broth (room temperature)
  • 1 cup evaporated milk (room temperature)
  • 1 cup heavy cream (room temperature)
  • 3 cups cheese ( 1 ¼ cup Smoked Gouda, 1 ¼ cup Gruyere and ½ cup grated parmesan)
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 ½ teaspoon Li Bon All Purpose seasoning

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees. Grease a 2-3 quart baking dish with unsalted butter.
  2. Peel potatoes and slice into ½ inch thick slices with a mandolin. Place slices in cold water to prevent browning.
  3. In a medium pot on low to medium heat, melt butter.
  4. Add onions, garlic and cook until fragrant.
  5. Whisk in flour and cook for about 2 minutes.
  6. Slowly whisk in chicken broth until smooth
  7. Whisk in evaporated milk followed by heavy cream until smooth.
  8. Continue to whisk on low heat, do not let it boil.
  9. Whisk in ½ of the grated parmesan (about 1/4 cup) until combined.
  10. Mix shredded gouda and Gruyere together and take 1 cup of it and stir it into the sauce.
  11. Add potatoes to the pot and mix so all of the potatoes are coated with the sauce.
  12. Place half of the potatoes into a layer in the buttered dish and top with half of the remaining parmesan and shredded cheese.
  13. Place the last of the potatoes into a second layer and top with the remaining parmesan and shredded cheese
  14. Cover tightly with foil paper and bake on 325 degrees on the middle rack for 1 hour.
  15. Remove foil, increase the oven to 350 degrees and continue baking for another 20 minutes, the cheese should be golden.
  16. Remove from the oven and let sit for 20 minutes.
  17. Garnish fresh chives and serve hot!
